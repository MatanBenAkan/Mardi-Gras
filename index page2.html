What Is Mardi-Gras?

Mardi Gras (mɑrdi ɡrɑ), or Fat Tuesday, refers to events of the Carnival celebration, beginning on or after the Christian feasts of the Epiphany (Three Kings Day) and culminating on the day before Ash Wednesday, which is known as Shrove Tuesday. Mardi Gras is French for "Fat Tuesday", reflecting the practice of the last night of eating rich, fatty foods before the ritual Lenten sacrifices and fasting of the Lenten season.

Our history

The source of the holiday is the carnival celebration. The carnival is celebrated as a Christian event, but the roots of the carnival can be traced back to Christianity in the Greek celebrations in honor of Dionysus and in the Roman celebrations in honor of Sternalia. The word "carnival" means "removal of the flesh" or parting from the flesh, In Europe, Catholic Christians used to abstain from meat and all other bodily pleasures for 40 days or six weeks before Easter, in identification with the suffering of Christ, a period known as the 'Lent'. Before the meat fast, there were celebrations of gluttony and sins, which in Italy, Switzerland, France, Portugal and Spain were accompanied by street parties of dances, costumes and masks.


The holiday in New Orleans

The holiday was brought to New Orleans by the French French founders as a religious holiday. The holiday soon became a non-religious cultural feature, and is celebrated mainly in the tourist area of ​​the French Quarter in the city. During the holiday, the streets of New Orleans are filled for about two weeks with unstoppable celebrations of dances, marches and intoxication, in particularly colorful costumes and clothing, and sometimes in partial attire by the participants in the festival.


LaToya Cantrell-Mayor of New Orleans

LaToya Cantrell (born April 3, 1972) is an American politician serving as the Mayor of New Orleans, Louisiana, a post she has held since May 7, 2018. Cantrell, a Democrat, is the first woman to hold the post. Before becoming mayor, Cantrell represented District B on the New Orleans City Council from 2012–2018.
Cantrell was inaugurated as mayor on May 7, 2018, the first woman to hold the position in the city's history. Once in office, she established a new Office of Youth and Families, with the goal of creating a strategic plan to address families in crisis in the city. Cantrell also founded a Gun Violence Reduction Council tasked with finding solutions to violent crime. Starting with a push to rededicate hotel taxes collected within the city for city use, Cantrell has focused on her #fairshare initiative to improve city infrastructure, public transportation, public parks, and green spaces. As part of that initiative, in October 2018 the City of New Orleans filed a lawsuit against four opioid manufacturers and distributors.
